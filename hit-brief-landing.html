The Problem

At the executive level, technology decisions rarely fail for technical reasons.

They fail because the people accountable for the outcome are handed vendor decks instead of judgment — noise instead of a position. By the time the risk is visible on the balance sheet, the decision has already been made.

Vendor noise, not counsel

Five competing pitches, no independent read on which one survives contact with your business.

M&A blind spots

Integration risk, security exposure, and system debt surface after the deal closes — not before.

Spend without leverage

Budgets grow while capability doesn't. No one owns the question of what to stop paying for.

The HIT Methodology

Harmonize. Integrate. Transform.

Three moves, in order. Skip the first two and transformation becomes a rebuild. HIT™ is the discipline that keeps technology decisions defensible from the boardroom down.

01 — Harmonize

Get the truth aligned.

Reconcile what leadership believes, what the systems actually do, and what the numbers say — before a single recommendation is made.

02 — Integrate

Make the parts one system.

Connect strategy, security, vendors, and operations so the decision holds together instead of solving one problem and creating three.

03 — Transform

Move with a defensible plan.

Act on a sequenced path the board signed off on — measured, reversible where it should be, and owned end to end.
